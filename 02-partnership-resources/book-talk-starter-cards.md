# Book Talk Starter Cards

A set of conversation prompts to help reading partners discuss books more meaningfully. Use these cards during partner meetings to guide discussions about plot, character, recommendations, and personal connections.

## How to Use
1. Print and cut out the cards.
2. Place them in a central location during partner meetings.
3. Partners can choose a card to start their conversation or use them when they feel stuck.

## Prompt Cards

### Character & Motivation
- **What does the main character want more than anything? What obstacles stand in their way?**
- **Which character would you want as a friend? Why?**
- **If you could give one piece of advice to a character, what would it be?**

### Plot & Conflict
- **What was the most exciting or surprising moment in the section you read?**
- **What problem does the main character face? How would you solve it differently?**
- **Predict what will happen next. What clues in the story support your prediction?**

### Theme & Connections
- **What lesson or big idea is the author trying to share?**
- **Does this story remind you of something from your own life, another book, or a movie?**
- **What questions would you ask the author if you could meet them?**

### Recommendations & Opinions
- **Would you recommend this book to a friend? Why or why not?**
- **What rating (1-5 stars) would you give this book so far? Explain your rating.**
- **What kind of reader would enjoy this book most? (e.g., someone who likes adventure, mystery, realistic stories)**

### Extend the Discussion
- **If you were to write a sequel, what would happen next?**
- **Choose a scene and describe how you would illustrate it.**
- **What music or soundtrack would fit this part of the book?**
